## 1. How to use this document

This is the content operating plan. Every article has a defined funnel stage, target ICP, monetization angle, and internal link targets. Use it three ways:

1. **As a publishing queue.** Pick the next article by stage priority and ICP coverage.
2. **As a monetization map.** Each article names its primary revenue lever (affiliate, quote lead, PDF sale, sponsor, tool CTA).
3. **As a briefing document.** When you or a writer sits down to draft, the row for that article has enough context to write from scratch.

Article target: **1,500 to 2,500 words** at expert-human voice standard (see the top 4 rewrites in `data/posts.json` for the reference bar).

### 2b. The six-layer funnel

```
Layer 1 — Awareness         Curious. No project. TOFU. Cheap traffic, high email capture value.
Layer 2 — Interest          Starting to think. First-principles. Basic education.
Layer 3 — Consideration     Comparing options. MOFU. Highest volume of long-tail queries.
Layer 4 — Intent            Specifying the project. Vetting. Ready to short-list builders.
Layer 5 — Decision          Buying. BOFU. Quote reviews, contract negotiation, financing.
Layer 6 — Retention         Post-purchase. Maintenance, upgrades, resale prep.
```

## 5. Video content plan (30 concepts + Claude Code motion stack)

### 5a. The Claude Code motion stack

Claude Code can build programmatic video several ways. Recommended stack:

| Tool | Best for | Ship complexity |
|---|---|---|
| **Remotion** | React-based programmatic video, data-driven scenes, social reels | Medium — full React app renders to MP4 |
| **Motion Canvas** | TypeScript-native, timeline-based animation, educational content | Medium — steep learning curve, powerful |
| **CSS/SVG animation → HTML2Canvas → FFmpeg** | Simple cover animations, social posts | Low — quick wins |
| **Manim (via Python subprocess)** | Mathematical/technical explainers | Medium — great for cost breakdowns |
| **Puppeteer + FFmpeg** | Screen recording our own tools/calculators | Low — record the cost calc running |
| **D3.js + Playwright → video** | Data journalism, cost report videos | Medium |

**Recommended primary stack:** Remotion (for social reels + explainers) + Puppeteer/FFmpeg for tool walkthroughs. Both integrate cleanly with our existing Next.js + TypeScript stack.

## Appendix B — Style rules (all articles)

Non-negotiable:

- Zero em-dashes. Use periods, commas, or "and" / "but".
- No AI tells: no "in fact", "moreover", "essentially", "ultimately", "let me explain".
- Specific numbers, not adjectives. "$14,500 median" beats "affordable".
- Named agencies, neighborhoods, storms, and contractors.
- Contractions throughout (it's, they're, you'll).
- Short sentences beat long. 12 to 20 words is the sweet spot.
- Every article has one Tampa-specific example, one number, one "reality check" callout.
- Every article ends with either "The bottom line" summary or a CTA-driven closer.
- Every article has one soft CTA mid-content and one hard CTA at the end.
