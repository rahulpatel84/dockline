## 1. Strategic Reset — What Changes and Why

### The problem with the current site
Right now the site publishes a **public** directory of builders. That undermines the entire lead-capture business model — visitors bypass our qualification form and go straight to a builder, and we make nothing. Worse, it trains people to see us as "just another Angi."

### The new positioning
> **MyDockGuide is the honest, deeply local resource that walks Tampa Bay waterfront owners from "should I build a dock?" to "here's exactly what happens next." When they're ready, we're the trusted matchmaker — not a phonebook.**

### Concrete changes we're making
- **Hide the public builder directory** (make it a back-office system, no more `/directory` page in nav or sitemap)
- **All roads lead to the quote form** — every guide, tool, and calculator has a matched quote CTA
- **Double down on educational content** — 100+ articles across permits, cost, materials, storm prep, ROI, insurance, and lifestyle
- **Build 10 interactive calculators and tools** — the moat is *utility*, not opinions
- **Programmatic location pages** — one page per Tampa Bay neighborhood + expansion across FL coast + comparable coastal/lakefront markets
- **Add high-authority verticals** — hurricane rebuild, flood insurance, waterfront ROI, luxury waterfront lifestyle — where the search intent is *high-value* and the competitor SEO is *thin*

### Why this wins
- **Every existing dock-related site is either a builder website (thin content, salesy) or a big-box aggregator (Angi, HomeAdvisor — spammy, national, no local depth).**
- No one owns the "waterfront educator" space in Tampa.
- Tampa Bay has ~200,000 waterfront homes. Average dock lifecycle: 15–20 years → ~10,000+ builds/rebuilds per year → market opportunity of $150M+ annually just in Tampa Bay.
- **Related verticals are massive**: seawalls ($30k avg), boat lifts ($15k avg), storm rebuilds ($40k+ avg). One qualified lead is worth $100–$400 to a builder.

## 2. Positioning & Brand Narrative

### Brand promise
Straight talk about docks. No fluff, no upsell, no fine print.

### Elevator pitch (30 sec)
> MyDockGuide is Tampa Bay's independent field guide to docks, seawalls, and boat lifts. We publish the honest, plain-English answers waterfront owners actually need — permits, costs, materials, storm prep, insurance — and when you're ready to build, we quietly match you with 2–3 vetted, licensed local builders. Free. No spam.

### Tone of voice
- **Plain-English > jargon.** "Piling" ok; "diaphragm-tied helical anchor system" — no.
- **Local flavor.** Reference Tampa neighborhoods, Bay hurricanes, marine wildlife, specific agencies by name.
- **Numbers over adjectives.** "$14,500 median" beats "affordable."
- **Empathy over authority.** We're a friendly neighbor, not the city inspector.
- **Never salesy.** The CTA is always secondary to the answer.

### Words we use
Waterfront · plain-English · honest · vetted · licensed · reviewed · Bay-tested · storm-ready · saltwater-grade · insider · homeowner-first

### Words we avoid
Cheap · deal · guaranteed · trust us · industry-leading · #1 · revolutionary

## 3. Target Personas

### Primary personas (95% of leads)

**Persona 1 — "Newly-Waterfront Nate" (35%)**
- Just bought a waterfront home in Tampa/Apollo Beach/Clearwater ($800K–$2.5M)
- 38–55, dual-income professional or entrepreneur, has kids or grandkids
- Dock is inherited (worn) or missing entirely
- Doesn't know one permit from another; scared of getting ripped off
- **Search intent:** "how much does a dock cost in Tampa," "do I need a permit," "floating vs fixed dock"
- **Content match:** Cost calculator, permit checklist, Getting Started guide bundle

**Persona 2 — "Storm-Rebuild Sarah" (25%)**
- Long-time Tampa Bay homeowner (60s, retiree or semi-retired)
- Dock/seawall damaged by hurricane (Ian, Milton, Helene, or next season)
- Filing insurance claim, dealing with permits, trying to rebuild before boat season
- **Search intent:** "seawall damage after hurricane," "dock insurance claim Florida," "FEMA flood zone rebuild rules"
- **Content match:** Storm rebuild cluster, insurance guides, contractor vetting

**Persona 3 — "Upgrader Ursula" (20%)**
- Lives on the water 5+ years, existing dock is functional but tired
- Wants to add a boat lift, upgrade to composite decking, or extend for a new boat
- Higher discretionary spend, quality-focused
- **Search intent:** "composite dock cost," "boat lift installation Tampa," "Trex vs Azek dock decking"
- **Content match:** Materials cluster, boat lift cluster, "worth it?" ROI content

**Persona 4 — "Investment-Property Ian" (10%)**
- Owns 1–3 waterfront rentals (Airbnb, VRBO) in Clearwater/Treasure Island/Anna Maria
- Docks are amenity + revenue driver
- ROI-focused, wants durability with minimal maintenance downtime
- **Search intent:** "does a dock increase rental income," "waterfront Airbnb amenities," "low-maintenance dock materials"
- **Content match:** ROI cluster, low-maintenance materials, luxury features

**Persona 5 — "Future-Buyer Fiona" (5%)**
- Actively house-hunting waterfront in Tampa Bay (or dreaming from out of state)
- Reading everything about waterfront ownership before pulling the trigger
- Long tail — 6–18 month lead cycle, but high LTV once they buy
- **Search intent:** "how much does waterfront cost in Tampa," "hidden costs of waterfront homes," "is Tampa waterfront a good investment"
- **Content match:** Waterfront buying cluster, luxury lifestyle, ROI, insurance

### Secondary personas (5% — long-tail traffic + brand halo)

- **DIY-Dave** — small handyman jobs, googling permit rules (low-value but great link building)
- **Marine-Contractor Manny** — competitor / potential partner, drives our directory revenue
- **Realtor Rachel** — waterfront real estate agent looking for content to share with clients (partnership channel)

- Calculator logic in a client-side React component
- Results PDF generated with `@react-pdf/renderer` or server-side Puppeteer

## 9. Lead Capture Funnel & Qualification Model

### The funnel

```
                    Organic search / social / paid
                                ↓
                    Article / Tool / Location page
                                ↓
              Soft CTA: "Get the free Planning Kit"
                                ↓
                         Email captured
                                ↓
              Nurture: 5-email onboarding sequence
                                ↓
              Progressive profile-building emails
                                ↓
              Trigger: user completes cost calculator OR
                       submits multi-step quote form
                                ↓
                    Lead qualification scoring
                                ↓
              Match to 2–3 vetted local builders
                                ↓
              Builders receive lead (they pay per lead)
                                ↓
              Homeowner receives builder outreach
                                ↓
              We track: contacted → quoted → signed
                                ↓
              Feedback loop → refine matching
```

### Multi-step quote form (replaces current /quote)

**Step 1 — Project type** (5 tiles: New dock / Repair / Boat lift / Seawall / Other)
**Step 2 — Size & scope** (dock length, boat size, etc.)
**Step 3 — Timeline** (Now / 3 months / 6 months / Just planning)
**Step 4 — Budget range** (Under $10k / $10–25k / $25–50k / $50k+ / Not sure)
**Step 5 — Materials preference** (Any / Wood / Composite / Aluminum / Not sure)
**Step 6 — Location** (ZIP → auto-fills county + reveals nearest match count)
**Step 7 — Contact** (name, email, phone, best time to call)

**Progressive reveal:** each step's completion increments a lead score. High-scoring leads are pushed to the top of the builder queue.

### Lead scoring model (0–100)

| Signal | Points |
|---|---|
| Project timeline < 3 months | +25 |
| Project timeline 3–6 months | +15 |
| Budget disclosed | +15 |
| Budget > $25k | +15 |
| ZIP in Tampa Bay Tier 1 | +10 |
| Phone number provided | +10 |
| Property owner (checkbox) | +5 (required) |
| Email domain personal (gmail etc.) | 0 |
| Email domain business/fake | -30 |
